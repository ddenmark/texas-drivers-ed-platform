# Module 10: Vehicle Requirements
**Target Duration:** 2.0 hours (120 minutes)  
**Position:** Module 10 of 12  
**Statutory Topics Covered:** Information Relating to Child Passenger Safety Seat Systems

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, students will be able to:
- Perform basic vehicle maintenance checks
- Understand required safety equipment in Texas
- Properly select and install child passenger safety seats according to Texas law
- Recognize the importance of vehicle safety features

## Lesson Breakdown

### Lesson 10.1: Basic Vehicle Maintenance (25 min)
- Tire pressure, tread depth, and condition
- Fluid checks (oil, coolant, brake, windshield washer)
- Lights and wipers
- **Interactive:** Pre-trip inspection checklist

### Lesson 10.2: Safety Equipment & Features (20 min)
- Seat belts, airbags, and their proper use
- Child safety locks and other features
- **Statutory Requirement:** Information Relating to Child Passenger Safety Seat Systems

### Lesson 10.3: Child Passenger Safety (40 min)
- Texas child restraint laws by age/weight/height
- Types of car seats and boosters
- Proper installation (LATCH system, seat belt routing)
- Common mistakes and how to avoid them
- **Interactive:** Car seat selection and installation simulator

### Lesson 10.4: Module Review & Assessment (35 min)
- Final assessment with emphasis on child passenger safety

## Key Teaching Points
- Texas law requires children under 8 to be in a child safety seat unless they are taller than 4'9"
- Proper installation dramatically increases effectiveness
- Many parents install seats incorrectly

## Sample Assessment Items
1. At what age/height can a child legally ride without a booster in Texas?
2. What is the LATCH system?
3. Name three common car seat installation mistakes.
