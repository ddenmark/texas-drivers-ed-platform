# After Course Completion: DPS Appointment & Next Steps

**Congratulations on completing your Texas DriveEd course!**

Here’s exactly what you need to do next to get your Texas driver license.

---

## Step 1: Download Your Certificate

After passing the final assessment, download your **DE-964** certificate from your student dashboard.  
You will need this document for your DPS appointment.

---

## Step 2: Gather Required Documents

For your DPS appointment, bring:

**For Learner Permit (if not already obtained):**
- DE-964 certificate (Module 1 completion is sufficient)
- Proof of identity (birth certificate, passport, etc.)
- Proof of Texas residency (2 documents)
- Social Security card or proof of SSN
- Payment

**For Provisional License / Driving Test:**
- Full DE-964 certificate
- Valid learner permit
- Proof of identity and residency
- Impact Texas Teen Drivers (ITTD) certificate (if required)
- Payment for license

---

## Step 3: Schedule Your DPS Appointment

1. Go to the Texas DPS website: [dps.texas.gov](https://www.dps.texas.gov)
2. Create or log into your account
3. Schedule an appointment at your local driver license office
4. Select the appropriate service (First-time driver license, etc.)

**Tip:** Appointments can fill up quickly. Schedule as soon as you have your documents ready.

---

## Step 4: Complete Any Remaining Requirements

Before your driving test, make sure you have:
- Held your learner permit for at least **6 months**
- Completed **44 hours** of behind-the-wheel practice (documented)
- Watched the **Impact Texas Teen Drivers (ITTD)** video (if under 18)

---

## Step 5: Take the Driving Skills Test

At your DPS appointment you will:
- Take a vision test
- Take the driving skills test (if ready)
- Pay any remaining fees
- Receive your provisional driver license (if you pass)

---

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I drive before getting my permit?**  
No. You must have a valid learner permit to practice driving.

**Q: What if I fail the driving test?**  
You can reschedule. There may be a waiting period and additional fees.

**Q: Do I need to bring my parent to DPS?**  
For minors, a parent or guardian signature is usually required.

---

## Need Help?

- Technical issues with your certificate: support@texasdrived.com
- DPS questions: Contact your local driver license office or visit dps.texas.gov
- Behind-the-wheel questions: Talk to your parent instructor or driving school
