# Module 9: Adverse Conditions
**Target Duration:** 2.0 hours (120 minutes)  
**Position:** Module 9 of 12  
**Statutory Topics Covered:** Hospital and Rehabilitation Facilities

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, students will be able to:
- Handle common vehicle emergencies (tire blowout, brake failure, etc.)
- Recover from skids and hydroplaning
- Know what to do immediately after a crash
- Understand the long-term human impact of serious crashes (hospital and rehabilitation)

## Lesson Breakdown

### Lesson 9.1: Vehicle Emergencies (30 min)
- Tire blowout procedure
- Brake failure and steering failure
- Engine failure and stalling
- **Interactive:** Emergency procedure decision trees

### Lesson 9.2: Skid Recovery & Hydroplaning (25 min)
- Types of skids and how to recover
- Hydroplaning causes and prevention/recovery
- **Interactive:** Skid recovery simulation

### Lesson 9.3: What to Do After a Crash (25 min)
- Immediate safety steps (move to safety if possible)
- Calling 911 and providing information
- Exchanging information with other drivers
- When to move the vehicle
- Texas crash reporting requirements

### Lesson 9.4: Human Impact of Crashes (25 min)
- **Statutory Requirement:** Hospital and Rehabilitation Facilities
- Stories from crash victims and first responders
- Long-term physical and emotional consequences
- The ripple effect on families and communities

### Lesson 9.5: Module Review & Assessment (15 min)
- Final Module 9 Assessment

## Key Teaching Points
- Most drivers will experience at least one vehicle emergency in their lifetime
- Knowing what to do after a crash can save lives and reduce legal complications
- The human cost of crashes is often underestimated by new drivers

## Sample Assessment Items
1. What should you do first if you have a tire blowout?
2. When is it okay to move your vehicle after a crash in Texas?
3. Why is it important to understand the long-term effects of serious crashes?
