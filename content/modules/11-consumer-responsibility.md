# Module 11: Consumer Responsibility
**Target Duration:** 2.0 hours (120 minutes)  
**Position:** Module 11 of 12  
**Statutory Topics Covered:** Texas Department of Insurance, Information Relating to Anatomical Gifts

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, students will be able to:
- Understand basic vehicle buying and leasing considerations
- Know Texas minimum insurance requirements and how to shop for coverage
- Understand the role of the Texas Department of Insurance (TDI)
- Learn about anatomical gifts and the importance of organ donation

## Lesson Breakdown

### Lesson 11.1: Buying & Leasing a Vehicle (25 min)
- New vs. used considerations
- Researching reliability and safety ratings
- Financing basics and total cost of ownership

### Lesson 11.2: Texas Insurance Requirements (30 min)
- Minimum liability insurance requirements in Texas
- What different types of coverage actually mean
- How to shop for insurance and compare quotes
- **Statutory Requirement:** Texas Department of Insurance

### Lesson 11.3: Understanding Your Insurance (20 min)
- Reading a policy
- Filing a claim process
- How claims affect future rates
- SR-22 and financial responsibility

### Lesson 11.4: Anatomical Gifts & Organ Donation (25 min)
- **Statutory Requirement:** Information Relating to Anatomical Gifts
- What anatomical gifts are and how they work
- The Texas Donor Registry
- Common myths vs. facts
- How to register as a donor
- **Interactive:** Organ donation awareness discussion

### Lesson 11.5: Module Review & Assessment (20 min)
- Final Module 11 Assessment

## Key Teaching Points
- Texas requires drivers to carry liability insurance
- Shopping around for insurance can save significant money
- Organ donation can save multiple lives — registration is simple

## Sample Assessment Items
1. What is the minimum insurance required to drive legally in Texas?
2. What does the Texas Department of Insurance do?
3. How can someone become an organ donor in Texas?
