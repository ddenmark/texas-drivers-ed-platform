# Module 4: Driver Readiness
**Target Duration:** 2.0 hours (120 minutes)  
**Position:** Module 4 of 12  
**Statutory Topics Covered:** None (foundational wellness module)

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, students will be able to:
- Assess their own physical and mental fitness to drive
- Recognize the effects of fatigue, illness, and medications on driving
- Understand the importance of vision and hearing for safe driving
- Make responsible decisions about when *not* to drive

## Lesson Breakdown

### Lesson 4.1: Physical Fitness to Drive (25 min)
- Vision requirements and common issues
- Hearing and its role in driving
- Effects of illness and injury
- **Interactive:** Vision and reaction time activities

### Lesson 4.2: Mental & Emotional Readiness (25 min)
- Stress, anger, and emotional state
- How emotions affect decision-making and risk perception
- Road rage awareness and management

### Lesson 4.3: Fatigue & Drowsy Driving (25 min)
- Signs of fatigue
- Microsleeps and their danger
- Texas statistics on drowsy driving crashes
- Strategies to combat fatigue

### Lesson 4.4: Medications, Alcohol & Other Substances (25 min)
- How prescription and over-the-counter drugs affect driving
- Combining substances (even legal ones)
- Personal responsibility for knowing your limits

### Lesson 4.5: Module Review & Decision-Making (20 min)
- "Should I Drive?" decision framework
- Final Module 4 Assessment

## Key Teaching Points
- Many medications carry warnings about driving
- Fatigue is as dangerous as alcohol impairment
- Young drivers often underestimate their level of impairment

## Sample Assessment Items
1. Name three signs that you might be too tired to drive safely.
2. Why can some cold medicines be dangerous while driving?
3. What should you do if you feel angry or stressed before driving?
