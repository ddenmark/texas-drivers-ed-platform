# Module 1: Traffic Laws
**Target Duration:** 6 hours (360 minutes)  
**Position:** First module (enables early learner permit eligibility)  
**Statutory Topics Covered:** Information Relating to Traffic Stops

## Learning Objectives (POI-DE Aligned)
By the end of this module, students will be able to:
- Explain the Texas Graduated Driver License (GDL) system and requirements for learner permit and provisional license
- Identify and interpret common traffic signs, signals, and pavement markings
- Apply right-of-way rules at intersections, 4-way stops, and yield situations
- Understand basic speed limits, following distances, and core rules of the road
- Describe the legal and practical consequences of traffic violations
- Demonstrate proper behavior and expectations during a traffic stop

## Lesson Breakdown (Recommended Flow)

### Lesson 1.1: Welcome & Texas Licensing Pathways (25 min)
- Course overview and how time tracking works
- GDL system explained (Learner Permit → Provisional → Full License)
- Age requirements and restrictions
- Parent/Guardian role in PTDE
- **Interactive:** "What license stage am I in?" flowchart

### Lesson 1.2: Texas Driver Handbook Deep Dive – Signs & Signals (45 min)
- Regulatory, warning, and guide signs
- Traffic signals and pavement markings
- School zones, construction zones, and railroad crossings (intro)
- **Interactive Activity:** Road Sign Recognition Game (drag-and-drop matching)
- **Assessment:** 10-question sign identification quiz

### Lesson 1.3: Right-of-Way Rules (40 min)
- 4-way stops and uncontrolled intersections
- Yield signs and merging
- Emergency vehicles and school buses
- **Interactive:** Branching video scenarios ("Who goes first?")
- **Assessment:** 6 scenario-based questions

### Lesson 1.4: Core Traffic Laws & Speed Management (35 min)
- Basic speed laws and prima facie limits
- Following distance (3-second rule +)
- Lane usage and passing basics
- **Interactive:** "Choose Your Speed" decision simulator

### Lesson 1.5: Consequences of Violations & Your Driving Record (20 min)
- Points system, suspensions, and insurance impact
- Criminal vs. civil traffic offenses
- How violations affect teens differently

### Lesson 1.6: Traffic Stops – What to Expect (25 min)
- **Statutory Requirement:** Information Relating to Traffic Stops
- Why you might be stopped
- What to do (and not do) during a stop
- Your rights and responsibilities
- Body camera / dashcam context
- **Interactive Scenario:** "Traffic Stop Simulator"
- **Assessment:** 5-question knowledge check

### Lesson 1.7: Module 1 Review & Knowledge Check (30 min)
- Comprehensive review of all topics
- **Final Module 1 Assessment** (modeled on DPS written test – 20 questions)
- Passing threshold: 80%
- **Certificate Trigger:** "For Learner License Only" portion of DE-964 becomes available upon successful completion

## Key Teaching Points (Texas-Specific)

- Texas has some of the strictest GDL restrictions for teens in the U.S.
- "Move Over or Slow Down" law applies to emergency and construction vehicles
- School bus passing rules are strictly enforced with high fines
- Construction zone fines are doubled in many cases
- Zero tolerance for minors regarding alcohol (any detectable amount)

## Statutory Integration Notes
**Traffic Stops** content must be factual, neutral, and empowering. Emphasize safety and cooperation while accurately representing rights. Use official DPS guidance where possible.

- Discuss family rules around phone use and distractions even before driving
- Encourage practicing identifying signs while riding as a passenger
