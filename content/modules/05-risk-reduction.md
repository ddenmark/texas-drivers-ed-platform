# Module 5: Risk Reduction
**Target Duration:** 2.5 hours (150 minutes)  
**Position:** Module 5 of 12  
**Statutory Topics Covered:** Information Relating to Street Racing

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, students will be able to:
- Apply defensive driving principles (Smith System or equivalent)
- Identify and respond to hazards proactively
- Manage speed and following distance to reduce risk
- Understand the dangers and legal consequences of street racing

## Lesson Breakdown

### Lesson 5.1: Defensive Driving Principles (30 min)
- The Smith System (or modern equivalent)
- "Aim High in Steering," "Get the Big Picture," etc.
- Scanning and predicting hazards

### Lesson 5.2: Hazard Recognition & Avoidance (30 min)
- Common driving hazards (intersections, pedestrians, animals)
- Texas-specific hazards (deer, flooding, construction)
- **Interactive:** Hazard identification videos

### Lesson 5.3: Space Management & Following Distance (25 min)
- 3-second rule and adjustments for conditions
- Maintaining space around the vehicle (front, sides, rear)
- "Space cushion" concept

### Lesson 5.4: Speed & Risk (20 min)
- How speed affects crash severity and reaction time
- Texas speed limits and prima facie laws
- The relationship between speed and risk

### Lesson 5.5: Street Racing & Aggressive Driving (25 min)
- **Statutory Requirement:** Information Relating to Street Racing
- Dangers of street racing (to participants and bystanders)
- Legal consequences in Texas
- Peer pressure and decision-making
- **Interactive:** "Would You Race?" scenarios

### Lesson 5.6: Module Review & Assessment (20 min)
- Final Module 5 Assessment

## Key Teaching Points
- Most crashes are caused by driver error, not vehicle or road conditions
- Increasing speed dramatically increases both crash likelihood and severity
- Street racing is not only illegal but often results in serious injury or death

## Sample Assessment Items
1. What is the Smith System?
2. How does following distance change in bad weather?
3. What are the consequences of street racing in Texas?
