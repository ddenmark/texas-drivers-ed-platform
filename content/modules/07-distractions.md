# Module 7: Distractions
**Target Duration:** 2.5 hours (150 minutes)  
**Position:** Module 7 of 12  
**Statutory Topics Covered:** Information Relating to Driving Distractions

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, students will be able to:
- Identify the different types of distracted driving (visual, manual, cognitive)
- Understand Texas laws and penalties related to distracted driving
- Recognize the real-world crash risks associated with distractions
- Apply practical strategies to minimize distractions while driving

## Lesson Breakdown

### Lesson 7.1: The Distracted Driving Epidemic (25 min)
- Texas and national statistics on distraction-related crashes
- Three types of distraction: visual, manual, cognitive
- Why teens are particularly vulnerable

### Lesson 7.2: Technology & Cell Phone Distractions (30 min)
- Texting while driving dangers and Texas law
- Hands-free vs. handheld debate
- App notifications and "Do Not Disturb While Driving"
- **Interactive:** Texting simulation (shows reaction time delay)

### Lesson 7.3: Other Common Distractions (25 min)
- Passengers, eating/drinking, grooming, reaching for objects
- In-vehicle technology (infotainment systems)
- Emotional distractions (road rage, arguments)

### Lesson 7.4: Prevention Strategies & Texas Laws (25 min)
- **Statutory Requirement:** Information Relating to Driving Distractions
- Texas Transportation Code penalties
- Personal commitment strategies
- Peer pressure resistance techniques
- **Interactive:** "What Would You Do?" scenarios

### Lesson 7.5: Module Review & Assessment (45 min)
- Final Module 7 Assessment
- Emphasis on real consequences and personal responsibility

## Key Teaching Points
- Texting while driving is more dangerous than driving drunk in many studies
- Texas has specific laws against using a wireless communication device while driving
- Even hands-free conversations can be cognitively distracting

## Sample Assessment Items
1. Which type of distraction is most dangerous when texting?
2. What is the primary danger of talking on a hands-free device?
3. Name three strategies to reduce distractions before starting the car.
