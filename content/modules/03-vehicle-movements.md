# Module 3: Vehicle Movements
**Target Duration:** 3.0 hours (180 minutes)  
**Position:** Module 3 of 12  
**Statutory Topics Covered:** Information Relating to Passing Certain Vehicles

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, students will be able to:
- Execute safe and smooth turns, lane changes, and merges
- Perform proper backing and parking maneuvers
- Safely pass other vehicles, including trucks and oversize vehicles
- Navigate roundabouts and complex intersections correctly

## Lesson Breakdown

### Lesson 3.1: Turning Procedures (30 min)
- Left and right turns from proper lanes
- Turn signals and shoulder checks
- Turning radius and path of travel
- **Interactive:** Turning path visualization

### Lesson 3.2: Lane Changes & Merging (30 min)
- Mirror, signal, shoulder check sequence
- Safe gaps and timing
- Merging onto highways
- **Interactive:** Lane change decision simulator

### Lesson 3.3: Backing & Parking (35 min)
- Straight line backing
- Angle and perpendicular parking
- Parallel parking technique
- 3-point turns
- **Interactive:** Parking simulator

### Lesson 3.4: Passing & Being Passed (30 min)
- **Statutory Requirement:** Information Relating to Passing Certain Vehicles
- When passing is legal and safe
- Passing trucks, buses, farm equipment, and oversize vehicles
- Being passed safely (move right, maintain speed)
- **Interactive:** "Should I Pass?" decision scenarios

### Lesson 3.5: Roundabouts & Complex Intersections (25 min)
- How to navigate single and multi-lane roundabouts
- Yield rules and lane selection
- **Interactive:** Roundabout navigation practice

### Lesson 3.6: Module Review & Assessment (30 min)
- Final Module 3 Assessment

## Key Teaching Points
- Many crashes occur during lane changes and merges due to poor observation
- Passing large vehicles requires significantly more time and space
- Roundabouts are statistically safer than traditional intersections when used correctly

## Sample Assessment Items
1. What is the proper sequence for changing lanes?
2. When is it illegal to pass another vehicle?
3. How should you behave when being passed by a large truck?
