# Texas DriveEd Student Handbook

**Welcome to Texas DriveEd**  
Your complete, TDLR-compliant online driver education experience.

---

## Course Overview

This course is designed to meet or exceed all requirements of the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation (TDLR) and the Program of Organized Instruction in Driver Education and Traffic Safety (POI-DE).

**Total Classroom Hours:** 32 hours  
**Behind-the-Wheel Requirement:** 44 hours (separate from this course)  
**Certificate Issued:** DE-964 (upon successful completion)

---

## How the Course Works

- **Self-Paced Learning:** You control your schedule, but minimum engagement time is enforced per lesson.
- **Interactive Content:** Videos, simulations, quizzes, and decision-making scenarios.
- **Progress Tracking:** Your progress is saved automatically. You can log in from any device.
- **Assessments:** Each module ends with a quiz. You must pass with 80% or higher to proceed in some sections.
- **Module 1 Completion:** After successfully completing Module 1, you will receive the “For Learner License Only” portion of your DE-964, allowing you to apply for your learner permit.

---

## Your Responsibilities as a Student

1. Complete all 12 modules and the final assessment.
2. Engage with the content for the required minimum time.
3. Take all quizzes and assessments honestly.
4. Review feedback and re-study areas where you struggled.
5. Be honest with yourself about your readiness to drive.

---

## Important Texas Laws & Reminders

- **Graduated Driver License (GDL) System**: You must follow all restrictions until you turn 18.
- **Learner Permit**: You may only drive with a licensed driver 21+ in the front seat.
- **Provisional License**: After holding your permit for 6 months and completing all requirements, you can take the driving test.
- **Zero Tolerance**: Any detectable amount of alcohol for drivers under 21 is illegal.
- **Distracted Driving**: Texas has strict laws against using wireless devices while driving.

---

## After You Finish the Course

1. Download your DE-964 certificate.
2. Schedule your DPS appointment.
3. Bring the following to DPS:
   - Your DE-964 certificate
   - Proof of identity and residency
   - Learner permit (if already issued)
   - Payment for license fees
