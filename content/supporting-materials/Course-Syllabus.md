# Texas DriveEd – Teen Driver Education Course Syllabus

**Course Title:** Texas Teen Driver Education (32-Hour Classroom/Theory)  
**Provider:** Texas DriveEd  
**Format:** 100% Online, Self-Paced with Enforced Time Tracking  
**Compliance:** TDLR-Approved Curriculum aligned to POI-DE  
**Certificate:** DE-964 upon successful completion

---

## Course Description

This course provides comprehensive classroom instruction in safe driving practices, Texas traffic laws, and defensive driving techniques. It is designed to meet or exceed all requirements set by the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation (TDLR) and aligns with the official Program of Organized Instruction in Driver Education and Traffic Safety (POI-DE).

Upon successful completion, students will receive a DE-964 certificate, which is required to apply for a Texas learner permit and provisional driver license.

---

## Course Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

- Demonstrate knowledge of Texas traffic laws, signs, signals, and pavement markings
- Apply defensive driving techniques and risk reduction strategies
- Understand the effects of alcohol, drugs, fatigue, and distractions on driving ability
- Recognize and respond appropriately to various driving environments and adverse conditions
- Practice safe interaction with other road users, including motorcyclists, pedestrians, and large vehicles
- Understand their legal and personal responsibilities as drivers in Texas
- Complete all 15 statutory topics required by Texas Education Code Chapter 1001 §§102–1101

## Course Structure

**Total Instructional Hours:** 32 hours (classroom/theory)

The course is divided into **12 modules**:

| Module | Title                              | Approx. Hours | Key Focus Areas |
|--------|------------------------------------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1      | Traffic Laws                       | 6.0           | GDL, signs, right-of-way, traffic stops |
| 2      | Driver Preparation                 | 2.5           | Vehicle controls, pre-trip inspection, seating |
| 3      | Vehicle Movements                  | 3.0           | Turns, lane changes, parking, passing |
| 4      | Driver Readiness                   | 2.0           | Fitness, fatigue, medications, decision-making |
| 5      | Risk Reduction                     | 2.5           | Defensive driving, hazard recognition, street racing |
| 6      | Environmental Factors              | 3.0           | Weather, construction zones, railroad crossings |
| 7      | Distractions                       | 2.5           | Technology, cognitive distractions, Texas laws |
| 8      | Alcohol and Drugs                  | 3.0           | Impairment, zero tolerance, refusal skills |
| 9      | Adverse Conditions                 | 2.0           | Emergencies, skids, post-crash procedures |
| 10     | Vehicle Requirements               | 2.0           | Maintenance, child passenger safety |
| 11     | Consumer Responsibility            | 2.0           | Insurance, buying a car, organ donation |
| 12     | Personal Responsibility            | 2.5           | Courtesy, sharing the road, human trafficking awareness |

**Note:** Behind-the-wheel training (44 hours) is **not** included in this course and must be completed separately (either through a parent instructor or professional driving school).

---

## Assessment & Completion Requirements

- Students must complete all modules and pass module quizzes (minimum 80% on most assessments).
- A comprehensive final exam must be passed at the end of the course.
- Minimum engagement time is enforced on all lessons to ensure instructional hours are met.
- Upon successful completion of the full course, students receive a **DE-964** certificate.

**Module 1 Special Note:** Successful completion of Module 1 allows students to receive the “For Learner License Only” portion of the DE-964, enabling them to apply for a learner permit.

---

## Student Expectations

- Engage honestly and actively with all course content.
- Complete lessons in the recommended order when possible.
- Review incorrect quiz answers and re-study as needed.
- Be honest about personal readiness to drive.
- Follow all Texas traffic laws during behind-the-wheel practice.
