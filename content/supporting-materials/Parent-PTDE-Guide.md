# Parent-Taught Driver Education (PTDE) Guide

**For Parents, Guardians, and Instructors**

Thank you for choosing Texas DriveEd to help your teen become a safe, responsible driver.

---

## Your Role as the Parent Instructor

As the designated parent instructor, you are responsible for teaching the **behind-the-wheel (BTW)** portion of driver education. This course covers the **classroom/theory** portion only.

**Your Responsibilities:**
- Supervise at least **14 hours** of structured behind-the-wheel instruction (7 hours driving + 7 hours observation).
- Log a minimum of **30 additional hours** of supervised practice driving (including 10 hours at night).
- Use the official TDLR PTDE Program Guide (purchased separately from TDLR).
- Model safe driving habits at all times.

---

## How This Online Course Helps You

This course prepares your teen with strong knowledge before they get behind the wheel. Key benefits:

- Module 1 completion allows your teen to apply for their learner permit early.
- All 15 statutory topics required by Texas law are covered.
- Interactive content improves retention and decision-making skills.
- Progress reports are available so you can track what your teen has learned.

---

## Recommended Teaching Approach

1. **Have your teen complete Module 1 first** → Apply for learner permit.
2. **Review key concepts together** before practicing on the road.
3. **Use the “Key Teaching Points” sections** in each module as discussion starters.
4. **Practice in low-risk environments** first (empty parking lots, quiet neighborhoods).
5. **Gradually increase complexity** as skills improve.

---

## Behind-the-Wheel Log Requirements

You must document:
- Date and time of each driving session
- Location and conditions
- Skills practiced
- Your signature as the instructor

Texas DriveEd provides downloadable BTW log templates in the student dashboard.

---

## Important Reminders

- You must hold a valid Texas driver license for at least 3 years.
- You should have a clean driving record (no recent suspensions or serious violations).
- Never allow your teen to drive if they are tired, distracted, or impaired.
- Lead by example — your driving habits will influence them for life.

---

## After Course Completion

Once your teen finishes the online course:
1. Download the full DE-964 certificate.
2. Complete the required BTW hours using the official TDLR PTDE Program Guide.
3. Schedule the driving skills test at DPS.
4. Your teen must also complete the Impact Texas Teen Drivers (ITTD) video.
