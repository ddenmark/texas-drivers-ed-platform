# Detailed Curriculum Mapping Template for TDLR POI-DE Approval

**Purpose:** This template helps you create the critical "Curriculum Mapping" document required for TDLR Driver Education provider approval. It demonstrates exactly how your course content, delivery methods, time allocation, and assessments cover every instructional objective in the official Program of Organized Instruction in Driver Education and Traffic Safety (POI-DE).

**How to Use:**
1. Obtain the latest official POI-DE document from TDLR (it lists specific modules and granular objectives with required instructional hours).
2. For each objective, fill in the corresponding row in your mapping.
3. Be specific: "Video 4:23 + Interactive Quiz (8 min average engagement)" is better than "Covered in Module 1".
4. Include total time verification that meets or exceeds POI-DE minimums.
5. Attach sample lesson content, quiz questions, and time-tracking methodology as appendices.

**Note:** The 12-module structure below is based on common POI-DE aligned provider structures (e.g., Traffic Laws as Module 1 for early permit eligibility). Expand or adjust to exactly match the official POI-DE module numbering and objectives.

## Modules 2–12 High-Level Mapping (Expand with POI-DE Objectives)

Use the same table format for each. Example structure:

**Module 2: Driver Preparation**
- Vehicle controls, instruments, pre-trip inspection
- Proper seating, mirror adjustment, blind spots
- Starting, stopping, steering techniques
- Texas vehicle inspection & registration requirements

(Allocate ~2.5–3 hours total. Include videos, interactive dashboard simulator, checklist downloads, quiz.)

**Module 3: Vehicle Movements**
- Turning procedures, lane changes, merging
- Backing and parking (all types)
- Roundabouts and complex intersections
- Passing safely

**Module 4: Driver Readiness**
- Physical and mental fitness
- Vision, hearing, fatigue, medications, distractions (cross-ref Module 7)
- Attitude and responsible decision making

**Module 5: Risk Reduction**
- Smith System or equivalent defensive driving
- Hazard recognition & avoidance
- Space management & following distance
- Speed management & risk

**Module 6: Environmental Factors** (Heavy Work Zone Emphasis)
- Weather & adverse conditions
- Night driving & glare
- **Construction / Work Zones (expanded 20–30 min dedicated + integrated scenarios)**
- Rural, urban, highway, and Texas-specific conditions (heat, flooding, etc.)

**Module 7: Distractions**
- Types and Texas statistics
- Cell phone / texting laws & penalties
- Passenger, tech, and cognitive distractions
- Strategies and Impact Texas Teen Drivers (ITTD) integration

**Module 8: Alcohol and Drugs**
- Effects on driving (BAC, impairment curves)
- Texas zero-tolerance, Open Container, DUI laws & penalties
- Social & legal consequences
- Refusal skills

**Module 9: Adverse Conditions**
- Vehicle emergencies (blowouts, brake failure, hydroplaning)
- Skid recovery & crash avoidance
- Post-crash procedures & Texas reporting requirements

**Module 10: Vehicle Requirements**
- Basic maintenance & safety checks
- Safety equipment (seat belts, child restraints — Texas laws)
- Legal modifications & equipment requirements

**Module 11: Consumer Responsibility**
- Buying, leasing, insuring a vehicle in Texas (minimum liability)
- Understanding insurance & shopping tips
- Environmental & community responsibility

**Module 12: Personal Responsibility and Driving**
- Courtesy, road rage, aggressive driving
- Sharing the road (motorcycles, bikes, pedestrians, trucks, buses, emergency vehicles)
- Lifelong safe driving habits & continuing education

**Final Assessment**
- Comprehensive exam covering all modules (80–100 questions, modeled on DPS test + higher-order scenarios).
- Course feedback + commitment statement.
- Full DE-964 generation + instructions for DPS / BTW next steps.

## Tips for Strong Submission
- **Be exhaustive** — TDLR reviewers want to see clear alignment, not "covered generally."
- **Quantify time** — Show exactly how you reach the required hours with verifiable methods.
- **Emphasize safety outcomes** — Link content to reducing crashes (Texas data, NHTSA).
- **Highlight innovation** — Interactive elements, work zone focus, parent tools, and robust compliance tech are differentiators.
- **Plan for iteration** — Expect TDLR feedback and be ready to revise mapping/content.
