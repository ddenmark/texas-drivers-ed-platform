# Compliant Texas Teen Driver Education Course Outline (POI-DE Aligned)

**Target:** TDLR-approved ~32-hour classroom/theory course for ages 14-17 (Parent-Taught or Self-Paced options).
**Structure:** 12 Modules + Final Assessment. Self-paced with enforced minimum engagement time per module.
**Key Feature:** Module 1 (Traffic Laws) completable first (~6 hours) → Issue partial certificate for learner permit application.
**Enhancements for 2026+:** Dedicated content or integrated sections on Construction/Work Zone Safety (dangers, penalties, safe practices per SB 1366) **and all 15 topics mandated by Texas Education Code Chapter 1001 §§102–1101**.
**Delivery:** Interactive online (video lessons, text, infographics, simulations/quizzes, Texas Driver Handbook references). Knowledge test at end of Module 1 and comprehensive final.
**BTW Note:** This course covers ONLY classroom. BTW (44 hours) handled via PTDE Guide or professional lessons.

**Statutory Compliance (Texas Education Code Ch. 1001 §§102-1101):** All 15 required topics are explicitly covered with dedicated time and assessments. See `statutory-required-topics.md` for full mapping and platform enforcement details.

## Module 1: Traffic Laws (~6 hours) - Permit Eligible
**Objectives (from POI-DE Module One):**
- Texas licensing process, GDL system, learner permit and provisional license rules.
- Basic traffic laws, signs, signals, pavement markings.
- Right-of-way rules, speed limits, following distances.
- Consequences of violations (points, suspension, insurance impact).
- **New:** Introduction to work zone laws and dangers.

**Content Ideas:**
- Welcome & How the Course Works (including time tracking, parent involvement for PTDE).
- Texas Driver Handbook Chapter 1-3 deep dive (with interactive sign quizzes).
- Video: "Your First Permit - What to Expect at DPS".
- Interactive: Road sign recognition game.
- Quiz: Module 1 Knowledge Check (must pass to unlock "For Learner License Only" cert portion? or practice test).
- Parent/Guardian resources & PTDE overview.

**Assessment:** Module quiz + readiness for DPS knowledge test.

**Certificate Trigger:** Upon successful completion → Generate "For Learner License Only" section of DE-964.

**Statutory Integration:** Information Relating to Traffic Stops

## Module 2: Driver Preparation
- Vehicle controls, pre-trip inspection, starting/stopping, steering.
- Seating position, mirrors, blind spots.
- Texas-specific vehicle registration, inspection (safety + emissions where applicable).

## Module 3: Vehicle Movements
- Turning, lane changes, merging, passing.
- Backing, parking (parallel, angle, perpendicular), 3-point turns.
- Roundabouts and complex intersections.

**Statutory Integration:** Information Relating to Passing Certain Vehicles

## Module 4: Driver Readiness
- Physical/mental fitness to drive (fatigue, illness, medications).
- Vision and hearing considerations.
- Attitude and decision-making.

## Module 5: Risk Reduction
- Defensive driving principles (Smith System or similar).
- Hazard recognition and avoidance.
- Space management, following distance (3-second rule +).
- Speed and its relation to risk.

**Statutory Integration:** Information Relating to Street Racing

## Module 6: Environmental Factors
- Weather (rain, fog, ice, wind, Texas heat/extremes).
- Night driving, glare.
- Road conditions (construction - expand here with new requirements).
- Rural vs urban vs highway driving in Texas.

**Statutory Integration:** Railroad and Highway Grade Crossing Safety | Oversize and Overweight Vehicles | Litter Prevention

## Module 7: Distractions
- Distracted driving epidemic (Texas stats).
- Cell phones, texting, passengers, eating, tech in vehicle.
- Strategies to minimize (Do Not Disturb, etc.).
- Impact Texas Teen Drivers (ITTD) video integration or reference.

**Statutory Integration:** Information Relating to Driving Distractions (full coverage)

## Module 8: Alcohol and Drugs
- Effects on driving ability (BAC, impairment).
- Texas laws (zero tolerance for minors, Open Container, DUI penalties).
- Designated driver, rideshares, consequences.
- Refusal skills and peer pressure.

**Statutory Integration:** Alcohol Awareness Information | Drug and Alcohol Driving Awareness Programs

## Module 9: Adverse Conditions
- Emergencies: tire blowout, brake failure, hydroplaning, skids.
- Crash avoidance and what to do after a crash (Texas reporting requirements).
- First aid basics? or emergency preparedness.

**Statutory Integration:** Hospital and Rehabilitation Facilities

## Module 10: Vehicle Requirements
- Maintenance basics (tires, brakes, lights, fluids).
- Safety equipment (seatbelts, airbags, child seats - Texas laws).
- Modifications and legal requirements.

**Statutory Integration:** Information Relating to Child Passenger Safety Seat Systems

## Module 11: Consumer Responsibility
- Buying/leasing a vehicle, insurance requirements in Texas (minimum liability).
- Shopping for insurance, understanding coverage.
- Environmental responsibility.

**Statutory Integration:** Texas Department of Insurance | Information Relating to Anatomical Gifts

## Module 12: Personal Responsibility and Driving
- Courtesy, road rage, aggressive driving.
- Sharing the road: motorcycles, bicycles, pedestrians, trucks, school buses, emergency vehicles.
- Lifelong learning, continuing education.
- Personal values and safe driving commitment.

**Statutory Integration:** Motorcycle Awareness Information | Human Trafficking Prevention Information

## Final Assessment & Certification
- Comprehensive final exam (covers all modules + statutory topics).
- Review of key concepts.
- Course completion survey/feedback.
- **Full DE-964 Certificate issuance** upon passing.
- Instructions for next steps.
