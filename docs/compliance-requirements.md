# Texas Driver Education Compliance Requirements (2026)

## Who Needs Driver Education in Texas?
- **Teens (Ages 14-17):** Required for learner permit and provisional license. Must complete before 18th birthday in most cases.
- **Adults (Ages 18-24):** Required 6-hour Adult Driver Education course before first license.
- **Adults 25+:** Not required, but recommended (can help skip written test at DPS in some cases).

## Key Regulatory Bodies
- **TDLR (Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation):** Approves providers, courses, issues licenses to schools/instructors. Oversees POI-DE curriculum standards. Handles certificate ordering and electronic completion reporting (new Online Licensing System).
- **DPS (Texas Department of Public Safety):** Issues learner permits, provisional licenses. Accepts TDLR-approved DE-964 (teen) and ADE-1317 (adult) certificates. Administers knowledge and driving tests.

## Classroom / Theory Requirements (Online OK if Approved)
- **Teens:** Aligned to **POI-DE (Program of Organized Instruction in Driver Education and Traffic Safety)**. Typically structured as ~32 hours total classroom instruction.
  - **Module 1: Traffic Laws** (~6 hours): Completion allows issuance of "For Learner License Only" portion of DE-964. Student can then apply for learner permit at DPS.
  - Remaining modules: Complete the full course.
  - **Methods:** Concurrent (start BTW earlier after Module 1) or Block.
  - Minimum time rules: Often max 2 hours classroom per day in some contexts; self-paced online must still meet total "hours" via timed content.
- **Adults 18-24:** 6-hour course per **POI-Adult Six-Hour**.
- **New Requirement (effective ~Sept 2026+):** All courses must include information on dangers, penalties, and safe driving in construction/maintenance work zones (SB 1366).

## Behind-the-Wheel (BTW) Requirements (NOT covered by online classroom course)
- **Teens:** 44 total hours supervised driving:
  - 14 hours structured with qualified instructor/parent: **7 hours driving instruction + 7 hours observation**.
  - 30 hours additional supervised practice (any licensed driver 21+), including **at least 10 hours at night**.
- Parent-Taught Driver Education (PTDE): Parent/guardian (or grandparent etc.) acts as instructor after purchasing official TDLR PTDE Program Guide. They must meet eligibility (valid TX license held 3+ years, clean record typically).
- Professional BTW lessons available from TDLR-approved driving schools.

## Certificates (Must be from TDLR-Approved Provider)
- **DE-964:** For teens / minor driver education (includes parts for DPS, insurance, school). Electronic reporting required.
- **ADE-1317:** For adult 6-hour course.
- Providers order certificate numbers via TDLR Online Licensing System and upload student completion data electronically (recent mandate; deadlines apply, e.g., within 15 days).
- Certificates must have unique control numbers; templates often pre-approved by TDLR.

## Provider Requirements to Offer Legal Courses
1. **Business:** Texas-registered entity (LLC recommended). Physical or virtual presence requirements?
2. **TDLR License/Application:** Apply to become Driver Education School/Provider. Submit:
   - Curriculum aligned to latest POI-DE / POI-Adult.
   - Instructor qualifications (for any live components; online may have different rules).
   - Business details, insurance?, background checks.
   - Fees (application + per course or annual?).
3. **Ongoing Compliance:**
   - Maintain records.
   - Allow TDLR audits.
   - Report completions electronically.
   - Update content for law changes (e.g., work zones).
   - Student identity verification and progress tracking to prevent fraud.
4. **For PTDE specifically:** Classroom portion via approved online provider + official PTDE Guide for parents (purchased separately from TDLR).

## Online Delivery Specifics
- Fully permitted if provider + specific course is TDLR-approved.
- Must enforce instructional time (can't let students skip to end in minutes).
- Include knowledge test (permit exam practice or proctored? Many allow online knowledge test as part of course).
- Mobile-friendly, accessible (ADA/WCAG recommended).
- Support for Spanish (Texas demographic).

## Sources & Verification
- Always verify current rules on official TDLR site: https://www.tdlr.texas.gov/ (search Driver Education).
- Check provider approvals via TDLR search tools.
- POI-DE is adopted by reference in 16 Texas Administrative Code (TAC) Chapter 84.
- Recent rule updates: April 2026 amendments, work zone additions.
