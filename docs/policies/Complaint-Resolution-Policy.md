# Complaint Resolution Policy

**Texas DriveEd**  
**Effective Date:** May 25, 2026

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes a fair and transparent process for receiving, investigating, and resolving complaints from students, parents, and other stakeholders.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to complaints regarding:
- Course content or delivery
- Technical issues with the platform
- Certificate issuance or delays
- Customer service interactions
- Billing or refund disputes
- Any other matter related to Texas DriveEd services

## 3. How to Submit a Complaint

Complaints may be submitted by:
- Email: support@texasdrived.com
- Through the student/parent dashboard (preferred for logged-in users)
- Phone: [To be added upon launch]

All complaints should include:
- Complainant’s name and contact information
- Description of the issue
- Relevant dates and details
- Desired resolution (if known)

## 4. Complaint Handling Process

1. **Acknowledgment**  
   We will acknowledge receipt of the complaint within 2 business days.

2. **Investigation**  
   The complaint will be investigated by the appropriate team member(s). This may include reviewing platform logs, course records, and speaking with involved parties.

3. **Resolution**  
   We aim to provide a written response with proposed resolution within 10 business days. Complex issues may require additional time; in such cases, we will provide status updates.

4. **Escalation**  
   If the complainant is not satisfied with the initial resolution, they may request escalation to a senior manager or the Compliance Officer.

## 5. Record Keeping

All complaints and their resolutions will be documented and retained in accordance with our Record Retention Policy.

## 6. Non-Retaliation

Texas DriveEd prohibits retaliation against any individual who files a complaint in good faith.

## 7. External Options

If a complaint cannot be resolved internally, complainants may contact:
- Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation (TDLR) – Education & Examination Division
- Texas Attorney General’s Consumer Protection Division

## 8. Continuous Improvement

Complaint trends are reviewed regularly to identify opportunities for improving our courses, platform, and customer service.
