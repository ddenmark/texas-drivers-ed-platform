# Student Enrollment Policy

**Texas DriveEd**  
**Effective Date:** May 25, 2026

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the requirements and procedures for student enrollment in Texas DriveEd driver education courses.

## 2. Eligibility

### Teen Driver Education (Ages 14–17)
- Must be at least 14 years old at time of enrollment
- Must be a Texas resident or planning to obtain a Texas driver license
- Parent or legal guardian consent is required for students under 18

### Adult Driver Education (Ages 18–24)
- Must be at least 18 years old
- Must be seeking a first-time Texas driver license (or as required by DPS)

## 3. Enrollment Process

1. Student (or parent/guardian) creates an account on the Texas DriveEd platform.
2. Identity verification is completed (name, date of birth, and contact information).
3. Payment is processed (or scholarship/voucher is applied).
4. Student gains access to the course dashboard.
5. Parent/guardian acknowledgment is required for students under 18.

## 4. Identity Verification

To protect the integrity of the certificate issuance process, Texas DriveEd may require:
- Upload of government-issued photo ID (for certificate issuance)
- Knowledge-based authentication questions
- Parent/guardian verification for minor students

## 5. Course Access

Upon successful enrollment, students receive:
- Immediate access to Module 1
- Ability to progress through modules at their own pace (subject to time requirements)
- Access to progress tracking, quizzes, and certificate upon completion

## 6. Multiple Enrollments

Students may only be actively enrolled in one Texas DriveEd course at a time. Duplicate enrollments may be canceled without refund.

## 7. Special Circumstances

- **Transfers:** Students may request to transfer their progress to a new enrollment under limited circumstances (e.g., technical issues). Approval is at the discretion of Texas DriveEd.
- **Scholarships / Vouchers:** Students using third-party funding must follow any additional requirements set by the funding organization.

## 8. Right to Refuse Enrollment

Texas DriveEd reserves the right to refuse enrollment to any individual who:
- Provides false or misleading information
- Has previously violated platform terms of service
- Appears to be attempting to circumvent course requirements
