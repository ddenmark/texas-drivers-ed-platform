# Record Retention Policy

**Texas DriveEd**  
**Effective Date:** May 25, 2026  
**Last Updated:** May 25, 2026

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the requirements for the retention, storage, and destruction of student records, business records, and other documents maintained by Texas DriveEd in compliance with Texas law, TDLR regulations (16 TAC Chapter 84), and best practices for educational providers.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all records created or maintained by Texas DriveEd, including but not limited to:
- Student enrollment and progress records
- Completion certificates (DE-964 / ADE-1317)
- Behind-the-wheel logs (for PTDE students)
- Financial and payment records
- Platform audit logs and system data
- Instructor and staff records (if applicable)
- Complaint and incident records

## 3. Retention Periods

| Record Type                          | Minimum Retention Period | Notes |
|--------------------------------------|---------------------------|-------|
| Student enrollment & progress records | 5 years from date of last activity | Includes course progress, quiz scores, and time logs |
| DE-964 / ADE-1317 Certificates       | 5 years from issuance     | Electronic + backup copies |
| Behind-the-Wheel (BTW) Logs          | 5 years from course completion | Required for PTDE students |
| Financial / Payment Records          | 7 years                   | Per IRS and Texas franchise tax requirements |
| Platform Audit Logs                  | 5 years                   | Includes time tracking and certificate issuance logs |
| Complaint & Incident Records         | 5 years from resolution   | Includes investigation notes |
| Instructor Records (if applicable)   | Duration of employment + 5 years | Background checks, qualifications, training records |

## 4. Storage Requirements

- All student records containing personally identifiable information (PII) shall be stored securely using encryption at rest and in transit.
- Electronic records shall be backed up regularly with off-site redundancy.
- Access to student records is limited to authorized personnel only, based on role-based access controls.
- Physical records (if any) shall be stored in locked, access-controlled locations.

## 5. Destruction of Records

Records shall be securely destroyed after the applicable retention period has expired using methods appropriate to the record type:
- Electronic records: Secure deletion / overwriting using industry-standard methods
- Paper records: Cross-cut shredding or professional document destruction services

A record of destruction shall be maintained, including date, record type, and method of destruction.

## 6. Legal Holds

In the event of litigation, investigation, or regulatory inquiry, relevant records shall be preserved beyond normal retention periods until the matter is resolved.

## 7. Compliance

This policy is designed to comply with:
- Texas Education Code Chapter 1001
- 16 Texas Administrative Code Chapter 84
- Texas Business & Commerce Code (identity theft and data breach provisions)
- Applicable federal laws (where relevant)

## 8. Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed at least annually or upon significant regulatory changes.
