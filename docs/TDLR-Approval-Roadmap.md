# TDLR Driver Education Provider Approval Roadmap (2026)

**Goal:** Guide to becoming a fully TDLR-approved Driver Education provider so you can legally offer courses and issue valid DE-964 / ADE-1317 certificates in Texas.

**Disclaimer:** This is a practical starter guide based on public information and regulations (16 TAC Chapter 84, TDLR site). Rules can change. Always verify directly with TDLR and consider consulting a regulatory attorney or compliance specialist familiar with driver education. This is not legal advice.

## Phase 1: Business & Legal Foundation (1-4 weeks)
1. **Form Texas Business Entity**
   - Recommend: Texas LLC (flexible, liability protection).
   - File Certificate of Formation with Texas Secretary of State.
   - Obtain EIN from IRS.
   - Register for Texas taxes if applicable (franchise tax, etc.).
   - Open business bank account.

2. **Research & Prepare for Application**
   - Visit TDLR Driver Education page: https://www.tdlr.texas.gov/driver/education/
   - Download or request latest application forms, Program Guides (POI-DE), and checklists.
   - Review 16 Texas Administrative Code (TAC) Chapter 84 in full.
   - Understand distinctions: Driver Education School vs. Parent-Taught program approvals.

3. **Key Requirements to Prepare**
   - Proof of business legitimacy.
   - Ownership and control information (background checks likely required for principals/instructors).
   - Physical or mailing address in Texas (virtual may be acceptable for pure online providers — confirm with TDLR).
   - Financial responsibility / insurance (if required for schools offering BTW or general liability).
   - Plan for record retention (student records, certificates, progress logs — minimum periods specified in rules).

**Estimated Cost:** $300–$1,000+ (formation fees, attorney if used).

## Phase 2: Curriculum Development & Mapping (4-12+ weeks — Critical Path)
This is the most important and time-consuming part.

**You must demonstrate that your course(s) meet or exceed every instructional objective in the official POI-DE (May 2022 or latest edition) and POI-Adult Six-Hour.**

### Steps:
1. **Obtain Official POI-DE Materials**
   - Contact TDLR Education & Examination Division or use provider portals once you have an account.
   - The POI-DE breaks down specific modules and granular objectives with required hours, topics, and performance standards.

2. **Create Detailed Curriculum Mapping Document**
   - For each POI-DE module/objective:
     - List your corresponding lesson/module.
     - Specify delivery method (video, interactive simulation, reading, quiz, discussion).
     - Allocate exact instructional time (must meet minimums; self-paced online uses verified engagement time).
     - Describe assessment method (quiz, scenario, final exam).
     - Note materials (Texas Driver Handbook references, custom videos, handouts).
     - Address new work zone requirements (SB 1366) explicitly.

3. **Develop Supporting Materials**
   - Full lesson scripts or detailed outlines.
   - Sample quizzes and final exams (with answer keys and passing standards, e.g., 80%).
   - Student/Parent Handbooks.
   - BTW logging templates (for PTDE compatibility).
   - Accessibility plan (captions, transcripts, WCAG 2.1 AA).

4. **Build Compliance Features in Platform (Parallel)**
   - Enforced minimum time per module/lesson (API-tracked watch time + reading time).
   - Sequential unlocking.
   - Identity verification flow.
   - Progress audit logs.
   - Certificate generation with unique control numbers (pre-approved template by TDLR).
   - Data export ready for TDLR Online Licensing System upload.

**Tip:** Start with a high-fidelity mapping for Module 1 (Traffic Laws) as it's the gateway for permits. Use the 12-module structure in `course-outline-texas-teen.md` as your base and expand it with objective-level detail.

## Phase 3: Instructor & Operational Readiness
- If offering any live instruction or oversight: Hire or qualify TDLR-licensed Driver Education Instructors (requires specific education/experience + licensing).
- For pure online/self-paced + PTDE support: Focus on platform compliance and customer support staff training.
- Develop policies:
  - Student enrollment & identity verification.
  - Refund / cancellation.
  - Data privacy (FERPA-like for student records + Texas privacy laws).
  - Complaint resolution.
  - Record retention & destruction.

## Phase 4: Submit Application to TDLR
1. Complete the official Driver Education Provider/School application form.
2. Attach:
   - Curriculum mapping + sample materials.
   - Business documents.
   - Instructor lists/qualifications (if applicable).
   - System descriptions (how you track time, issue certs, report to TDLR).
   - Fees (application fee + any per-course or annual fees — confirm current amounts).
3. Background checks / fingerprints for key personnel.
4. Possible on-site or virtual review/audit by TDLR.

**Timeline:** Application review can take several weeks to a few months. Be prepared for requests for additional information.

**After Approval:**
- Order certificate numbers via TDLR Online Licensing System.
- Begin offering courses.
- Maintain ongoing compliance (annual renewals?, audits, content updates for law changes).
- Report every student completion electronically within required deadlines (e.g., 15 days).

## Estimated Total Timeline & Investment
- **Minimum realistic:** 3–6 months to first approved course (if you move fast on curriculum).
- **More typical for high-quality online platform:** 6–12 months (content production, platform dev, mapping, review cycles).
- **Costs:** Business formation ($500+), legal/compliance consulting ($2k–$10k+), content production (videos, design — $5k–$50k+ depending on quality), platform development (MVP $10k–$100k+), TDLR fees, insurance.

## Recommended Next Actions Right Now
1. Form your LLC this week.
2. Contact TDLR (phone or webform) and request the latest application packet and POI-DE access info. Ask specifically about requirements for online-only providers.
3. Begin detailed curriculum mapping using the template/structure in this repo (start with Module 1 full mapping).
4. Decide on MVP scope: Start with Adult 6-hour course (simpler) or go straight for full Teen 32-hour.
